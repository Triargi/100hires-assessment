# Expert Selection Rationale

Topic: **LinkedIn organic content strategy for B2B SaaS**

The goal was to find practitioners with strong, specific expertise across the main parts of LinkedIn organic strategy: algorithm behavior, founder-led content, Company Pages, employee advocacy, positioning, AI-assisted content, B2B creativity, and marketing execution.

## Selection Criteria

Experts were chosen based on:

- Direct experience using LinkedIn as a serious B2B channel.
- Credibility from operating roles, products, research, or repeated public work.
- Clear connection to B2B SaaS or B2B marketing.
- Public content available through LinkedIn posts, YouTube, podcasts, articles, or webinars.
- Coverage of a distinct subtopic, so the research is not just 10 people saying the same thing.

## Expert Rationale

### Ty Heath

Ty brings the B2B brand and marketing science perspective. Her work through LinkedIn and The B2B Institute makes her useful for understanding long-term demand, mental availability, emotional storytelling, and why B2B content needs to be memorable.

### Richard van der Blom

Richard is included for LinkedIn algorithm and organic reach expertise. His Algorithm Insights reports and public interviews make him one of the strongest sources for distribution mechanics, posting behavior, profile signals, engagement, and format performance.

### Michelle J. Raymond

Michelle is included for LinkedIn Company Pages and B2B brand presence. She is especially useful because many LinkedIn organic strategies over-focus on personal profiles and ignore the Company Page as a trust and validation layer.

### Justin Welsh

Justin is included for personal branding, consistency, and audience-building systems. His experience building a business through LinkedIn organic content gives the project a strong practitioner view of repeatable content creation.

### Rob Illidge

Rob is included for employee advocacy and executive branding. His work with Vulse is directly relevant to B2B companies that want to scale LinkedIn content beyond one founder or company account.

### Alex Boyd

Alex is included for founder-led engagement and LinkedIn as a demand creation channel. His work with Aware/DemandBird is relevant to SaaS founders who want practical LinkedIn workflows, not only inspiration.

### MJ Smith

MJ is included for SaaS marketing leadership and real-world content operations. Her public content is useful for strategic narrative, CEO posting, launch storytelling, and turning internal expertise into public-facing marketing.

### Anthony Pierri

Anthony is included for SaaS positioning and product marketing. LinkedIn content quality depends heavily on positioning, segmentation, and market maturity, which makes his work important for turning vague posts into buyer-relevant content.

### Eva Johanna Egg

Eva is included for AI-assisted LinkedIn content workflows and authentic personal branding. Her work with Scripe is relevant to modern B2B SaaS teams trying to scale content without losing human voice.

### Wensy Antoli

Wensy is included for B2B creativity, video, and emotional storytelling. Her LinkedIn Creative Labs work helps cover the creative side of organic strategy: memorability, personality, and content formats beyond text posts.

## Why This Mix Is Strong

The expert set is intentionally balanced:

- Algorithm and reach: Richard.
- Company Pages and B2B brand presence: Michelle.
- Founder-led content: Justin and Alex.
- Employee advocacy: Rob.
- SaaS positioning and narrative: Anthony and MJ.
- AI content systems: Eva.
- B2B creativity and memorability: Ty and Wensy.

This gives the project enough breadth to support a real LinkedIn organic playbook later.
