## Research Summary

Rob lays out employee advocacy as a system rather than a one-off request for employees to share company content. A useful program needs clear business goals, chosen advocates, content themes, enablement, governance, measurement, and a launch sequence that starts small before scaling. The implied warning is that company-wide launches often fail when employees are treated as distribution channels rather than credible professional voices.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, this is a strong model for turning internal expertise into organic distribution. Start with a founder, one subject-matter expert, and one customer-facing employee. Give them a few recurring content pillars: buyer pain, product lessons, customer outcomes, industry shifts, and implementation insight. Then expand once the first cohort shows proof.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Start with a small advocate cohort, not the whole company.
- Give employees content pillars and examples, not rigid copy-paste scripts.
- Scale once the program has proof, cadence, and leadership support.
