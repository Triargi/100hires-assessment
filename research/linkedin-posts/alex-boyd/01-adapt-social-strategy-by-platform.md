## Short Excerpt

"I need to adapt my social media strategy."

## Research Summary

Alex explains that the same message should not be used in the same way across LinkedIn, Substack, Twitter/X, Bluesky, Threads, Mastodon, and YouTube. For LinkedIn, he says he will lean into DemandBird and continue being known as a "social media" person because that is what his audience and the algorithm appear to expect from him.

The post frames social media as behavior-driven rather than random. What a person engages with shapes what they see, and deliberate platform use determines whether the channel becomes useful or noisy.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, this supports a channel-specific LinkedIn strategy. LinkedIn should carry the founder's category authority, product narrative, and buyer education, while other platforms can serve different tones or audience segments. A SaaS founder should not simply cross-post the same content everywhere.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Match the message to the platform's audience and behavior.
- Use LinkedIn to reinforce the category you want to be known for.
- Let audience response shape focus without drifting away from the product narrative.
