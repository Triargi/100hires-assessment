## Short Excerpt

"Save me from the generic LinkedIn advice."

## Research Summary

Alex describes a SaaS founder whose growth has slowed because they have not yet learned the 80/20 of channels such as influencer marketing, affiliate programs, and community-led growth. He argues that what helps founders is not generic LinkedIn advice or theory with a polished hook, but real playbooks from people actively running SaaS companies.

The post is relevant to organic content strategy because it criticizes performative content and elevates practical operating knowledge.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, the strongest LinkedIn content should sound like field-tested insight, not recycled advice. Founders and operators can create more useful organic content by sharing actual channel learnings, mistakes, data, and decision criteria. This attracts serious buyers and peers because it provides operating clarity.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Publish concrete playbooks and lessons, not generic tips.
- Use proof, data, and firsthand experience to build authority.
- Make LinkedIn content useful enough that serious operators would save or discuss it.
