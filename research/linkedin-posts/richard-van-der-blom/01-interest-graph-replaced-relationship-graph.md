## Short Excerpt

"The algorithm you've learned about and optimized for died 18 months ago."

## Research Summary

Richard says LinkedIn has shifted from a Relationship Graph to an Interest Graph. Under the old model, distribution depended heavily on who followed or knew you. Under the newer model, LinkedIn routes content based on topic relevance, expertise signals, and what the platform believes each audience wants to see. The post cites research from 1.3 million posts and says creators with strong topic signals can sustain post longevity significantly longer.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, this means random posting weakens distribution. A company should define a tight set of category themes and repeat them consistently through founder, executive, employee, and company content. LinkedIn needs enough signals to understand what the brand and its experts should be known for.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Build repeatable topic authority instead of chasing broad reach.
- Align personal profiles, company page, and posts around the same category themes.
- Treat LinkedIn as an interest-routing system, not only a follower network.
