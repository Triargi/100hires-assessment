## Short Excerpt

"Your Profile is the clearest signal LinkedIn has."

## Research Summary

Michelle argues that LinkedIn is increasingly trying to understand what people are known for, not only who they are connected to. A profile gives LinkedIn, AI tools, search systems, and human buyers structured signals about credibility, expertise, audience, and relevance. If the profile is unclear, posting more can amplify confusion rather than improve results.

The article frames profile clarity as a foundation for content performance. Posts can change frequently, but a profile should anchor the audience, category, and proof behind the content.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, founder, executive, and subject-matter expert profiles should clearly match the company's category narrative. A SaaS CEO posting about customer pain should have a profile that reinforces the same ICP, problem, and credibility. Otherwise, buyers and the platform receive mixed signals.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Optimize key team profiles before increasing posting volume.
- Align profile headlines, About sections, Featured content, and recurring topics.
- Use profiles to make expertise legible to buyers, LinkedIn, and AI search systems.
