## Short Excerpt

"Company Pages were never meant to behave like Profiles."

## Research Summary

Michelle argues that many marketers misunderstand the role of LinkedIn Company Pages. Pages are often judged against personal profiles, then dismissed when they do not get the same feed engagement. Her view is that Pages still matter as validation points, trust signals, and participation channels, especially when buyers check a vendor before or during evaluation.

She also pushes teams to move beyond broadcast-only posting. A stronger Company Page shows up in comments, supports employee content, joins relevant conversations, and presents a clearer point of view.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, the Company Page should not be treated as a press release feed. It should validate the product category, explain who the SaaS is for, show proof, and support founder or employee-led content. This matters because SaaS buying committees often research the company independently before booking or approving a demo.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Measure Company Pages by validation, credibility, and participation, not only post reach.
- Combine Company Page content with employee and executive voices.
- Use the Page to comment, support, and participate in category conversations.
