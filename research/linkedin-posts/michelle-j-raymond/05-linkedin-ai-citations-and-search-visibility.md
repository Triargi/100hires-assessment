## Research Summary

Michelle responds to research showing LinkedIn is frequently cited by AI tools and warns marketers not to misinterpret the finding as a reason to simply post more. Her point is that AI and search systems rely on indexed, structured, credible information, not a live reading of the feed. Visibility depends on clarity, structure, relevance, and useful content that can be understood beyond the moment it is published.

For organic LinkedIn strategy, this widens the role of LinkedIn from feed distribution to discoverability and validation.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, LinkedIn content can support AI/search discoverability when company pages, profiles, articles, and posts consistently reinforce the same category language. SaaS teams should treat LinkedIn as part of their public knowledge graph: clear product categories, buyer pains, use cases, proof, and expert commentary all help buyers and AI systems understand the company.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Optimize LinkedIn for discoverability, not only feed engagement.
- Use consistent category language across profiles, Pages, and long-form content.
- Publish structured, useful explanations that support buyer research.
