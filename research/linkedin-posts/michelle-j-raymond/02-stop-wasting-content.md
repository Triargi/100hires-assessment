## Short Excerpt

"This isn't a content problem. It's a content waste problem."

## Research Summary

Michelle explains that many B2B teams are not failing because they lack content; they are failing because strong content gets used once, hidden in silos, or never activated by sales and employees. She recommends thinking in terms of a content system: one strong idea should travel across LinkedIn posts, newsletters, sales enablement, employee advocacy, video, and website content.

The post/article also emphasizes repetition. B2B audiences do not see everything a company publishes, so repeating key messages across formats is part of building recognition.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS, this is a practical operating model. A single product insight, customer proof point, research finding, or category POV can become multiple LinkedIn posts, founder posts, sales follow-up assets, newsletter snippets, and help-center or blog content. This reduces content waste and reinforces the product narrative across the buying journey.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Start with reusable core ideas, not one-off posts.
- Repurpose SaaS content across company, founder, sales, and employee channels.
- Repeat important messages until the market can remember them.
