## Short Excerpt

"This one's for the marketers that are sick of people throwing ideas over the wall at you."

## Research Summary

MJ challenges marketers to respond differently when people outside marketing bring random ideas. Instead of treating cross-functional input as an interruption, the post frames it as a chance to understand the business better and shape stronger strategy. The underlying content lesson is that good marketing does not come from isolated content calendars; it comes from translating business insight into clear external messages.

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS teams, product, sales, customer success, and leadership often hold the raw material for strong LinkedIn content. A marketer's job is to turn those inputs into coherent narratives, not simply reject them or publish them unchanged. This creates better company posts, founder posts, and employee-led content.

## Content Strategy Takeaway

- Treat internal requests as inputs to refine, not orders to publish.
- Build a workflow for turning stakeholder ideas into buyer-relevant content.
- Use marketing judgment to connect internal knowledge with external demand.
